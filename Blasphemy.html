"Blasphemy"
Photography
"Blasphemy" is an early attempt of mine in still-life photography. Using a mini bust of Michelangeloâ€™s David statue from Renaissance as the subject of shooting, this project aims to strike the classical artforms. Composited with tinfoils, a broken goblet, lace curtains, chandelier, and fires, the bust representing classical artforms collides with modern objects respresenting blaspheming acts on the elegance in ancient times.

I created a diptych as a conversation between old and new. I was trying to make a collision between classical and contemporary approach. They are in a horizontal composition because people usually unfold the chronicled timeline horizontally. David from the past is looking towards the right side where David from the present is located, as if he is looking firmly to his future. The combination of David and fires was my attempt to metaphorically express rebirth of classical arts in digital forms, as well as sparks classcial arts ignites for contemporary multi-media artists.
